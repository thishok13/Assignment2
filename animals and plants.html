Our Animals!

Giraffe

Giraffa camelopardalis

Giraffes, with their high legs and lengthy necks, are the world's tallest mammals. A giraffe's legs alone are roughly 6 feet long, making it taller than many humans. Giraffes can run as fast as 35 miles per hour over short distances and cruise at 10 miles per hour over extended distances thanks to their enormous legs.

Ostrich

Struthio camelus

The world's largest bird is the flightless ostrich. They live in African savannas and deserts, and the plants they eat provide them with the majority of their water.

Macaws

Psittacidae

Macaws are vividly colored parrots with exquisite plumage. With its green canopies and vivid fruits and flowers, the coloring is appropriate for life in Central and South American rain forests.

Bengal Tiger

Panthera tigris tigris

Tigers are the largest cats in the world, and they are known for their power and strength. Bengal tigers are sometimes known as Indian tigers because they inhabit in India. They are the most common tiger species, accounting for over half of all wild tigers.

Leopards

Panthera pardus

Leopards, like lions, tigers, and jaguars, are sleek and strong big cats. Many of their populations, particularly outside of Africa, are threatened. Because the leopard is so muscular and at ease in trees, it frequently drags its prey into the branches.

Ring-Tailed lemur

Lemur catta

Lemurs are primates that can only be found on the African island of Madagascar and a few nearby islands. The long, brilliantly striped black-and-white tail of ring-tailed lemurs makes them easily identifiable. Many zoos have them as permanent inhabitants.

Russel's Viper

Daboia russelii

It can be found in open terrain from India to Taiwan and Java. Because it generally lives in farmlands where human contact and rodent prey are plentiful, it is a leading cause of snakebite mortality within its range. Three rows of reddish brown markings are defined in black and then white on the viper.

Burmese Python

Python bivitattus

Burmese pythons are perhaps best recognized as the snake of choice among reptile owners for big reptiles, thanks to its gorgeously patterned skin, quick development rate, and typically gentle nature. Burmese pythons are among the world's largest snakes, native to Southeast Asia's jungles and grassy marshes.

Giant Indian Squirrel

Ratufa indica

One of the largest squirrels, the Indian gigantic squirrel has a distinctive one-, two-, or three-toned color scheme. Whitish, creamy-beige, buff, tan, rust, reddish-maroon, brown, a dark seal brown, or black are some of the colors that can be used. The underparts and front legs are often cream-colored, but the head might be brown or beige with a prominent white spot between the ears. Colors vary depending on the subspecies.

Our Plants

Picture Name Description
Satin Wood The rue tree Chloroxylon swietenia, also known as Ceylon satinwood or East Indian satinwood, is native to Southeast Asia, India, and Sri Lanka (Ceylon). Satinwood is collected for its durable yellowish brown wood with a satiny luster, which is utilized in exquisite cabinetry and farming implements. Traditional medicine uses many parts of the plant.
Bread Fruit The breadfruit tree (Artocarpus altilis (Parkinson) Fosberg) is a traditional agroforestry species that is evergreen and multipurpose. In the Pacific Islands, its starchy fruits are a staple meal. Breadfruit gets its name from the flavor of the cooked fruit, which is similar to that of freshly baked bread.
Jack Fruit The jackfruit (Artocarpus heterophyllus), often known as jack tree, is a fig, mulberry, and breadfruit tree species (Moraceae). The Western Ghats of southern India, all of Sri Lanka, and the rainforests of Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines are where it all began. The jack tree thrives in tropical lowlands and is commonly farmed throughout the world's tropical regions. It produces the most fruit of any tree.
Na Tree Mesua ferrea, often known as Ceylon ironwood or cobra saffron, is a Calophyllaceae plant. The weight and hardness of the wood give this slow-growing tree its name. Because of its graceful shape, grayish-green foliage with a lovely pink to scarlet flush of drooping young leaves, and huge, fragrant white flowers, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental. It's native to South Asia's damp, tropical regions. It is Sri Lanka's national tree.
Mango tree Mangifera indica, also known as mango, is a flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family. Mangoes are thought to have originated in the area between Myanmar, Bangladesh, and India in northern Myanmar. The mango is India's, Pakistan's, and the Philippines' national fruit, as well as Bangladesh's national tree.
Lantana Lantana is a genus of roughly 150 perennial flowering plants belonging to the Verbenaceae family. They are native to the tropical regions of the Americas and Africa, but they have been brought to a number of areas, including the Australian-Pacific region, as well as the southern and northeastern parts of India. The red, orange, yellow, or blue florets in Lantana's scented flower clusters (called umbels) are mixed with white florets. As new types are chosen, new colors emerge.
